About This Report

A data-driven reading of who lived and who died aboard the RMS Titanic on the night of 15 April 1912. The study covers 769 passengers across three classes and three boarding ports, asking one question four different ways. Chapter I weighs class against the lifeboats. Chapter II tracks the age profile of survivors against non-survivors. Chapter III compares the three boarding ports of Southampton, Cherbourg, and Queenstown. Chapter IV puts a price on safety, lining up the average fare paid by survivors against the fare paid by non-survivors in the same class. The pattern that surfaces is unforgiving. Wealth, class, and a head start in the gangway each tilted the night.
