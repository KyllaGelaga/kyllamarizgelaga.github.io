On April 15, 1912, the RMS Titanic sank in the North Atlantic after striking an iceberg. Of the 769 passengers in this dataset, only 152 survived — a grim 19.8% survival rate. The following chapters explore how class, age, port of embarkation, and fare shaped one's fate that night.

First-class passengers had the highest survival rate at 25.1%, while third-class passengers — though most numerous — had the lowest at 17.6%. The data echoes the era's social order, even at sea.
