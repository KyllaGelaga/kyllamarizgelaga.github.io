Shopee Camping Shoes: A 60-Listing Buyer's Audit

What we learned about prices, brands, ratings, and shipping when we pulled every camping shoe Shopee Philippines surfaced for one search.

What is Shopee?

Shopee is the largest e-commerce marketplace in Southeast Asia, owned by Singapore-based Sea Limited. In the Philippines it goes head-to-head with Lazada and TikTok Shop, hosting everything from electronics to outdoor gear.

Most listings are fulfilled either by Philippine-based sellers (next-day or two-day shipping out of Metro Manila and Bulacan) or by cross-border sellers in Mainland China (cheaper prices, four to eight day waits). Shopee layers on ratings, free-shipping vouchers, the ShopeePay digital wallet, and SPayLater, its buy-now-pay-later product that splits a purchase into 1, 3, 6, or 12 monthly installments.

What this project does

We scraped 60 Shopee PH listings for the query "camping shoes," cleaned the data, and built two deliverables on top of it.

1. A market-scan deck

Ten slides covering the price landscape, brand competition, top picks by review-weighted score, where listings ship from, and the risks buyers should watch for.

2. An interactive Excel dashboard

Type your budget, pick your terrain and fitness level, and the workbook ranks the affordable matches and shows what each SPayLater installment plan would cost.

What we uncovered

1

The mid-range is a dead zone, not a sweet spot

The ₱500 to ₱1,000 tier has the lowest average rating in the dataset (4.57★) and almost no buyer attention: 107 reviews across 7 listings. Buyers either buy cheap (under ₱500) or jump to the ₱1,000 to ₱3,000 premium tier. The middle gets skipped.

2

Camel and Camel Crown built a real premium

Together they hold 20 of the 60 listings at an average price of ₱2,043, and customers reward it: 4.90★ average rating across 13,000+ reviews. The premium positioning works on Shopee even when the next aisle is full of ₱200 alternatives.

3

"Unbranded" is a category, not a junk drawer

Twenty-three listings are tagged Unbranded, totaling 12,186 reviews and ₱860 average price. Volume play that works: lowest rating in the dataset (4.71★) but enormous customer reach. Cheap unbranded is how most buyers actually start.

!

Average ratings hide real complaints

Five listings carry 5%+ of their reviews at 1 to 2 stars. The worst hits 25% negative on a 4.3★, ₱309 unbranded shoe. The 4.78★ market average smooths over genuine quality risk that only shows up when you read the negative bucket.

4

Price barely predicts rating

Correlation between price and rating is only +0.26. Paying more nudges quality up, but not reliably. Our highest-rated listing sits at ₱858; our most-reviewed listing sits at ₱199. Cheap and good are not mutually exclusive on this catalog.

5

Half the catalog is a slow boat from China

21 of 60 listings ship from Mainland China (4 to 8 days). The 38 listings promising "Tomorrow" or "Less than 2 days" are almost entirely PH-based sellers in Bulacan, Valenzuela, and Pasig. Buyers who need gear this week are choosing from a much smaller pool than the catalog suggests.

How the analysis was built

Sixty listings scraped from Shopee PH for the query "camping shoes." Prices cleaned from peso-prefixed strings into numeric values. Each shoe tagged for terrain suitability (light trails, mixed hiking, rocky, wet) and fitness fit (beginner, regular, advanced) using keyword matching on the product title. Rankings use a Bayesian-weighted score that pulls small-sample ratings toward the dataset mean, so a 5.0★ shoe with 7 reviews does not outrank a 4.9★ shoe with 5,529.
